Quality Control with Computer Vision

Utilizing OpenCV and C++, I made a system that was meant for usage in small-parts quality control, specifically washers. The camera would be calibrated against a correct washer and the number of pixels across a correct washer was would be inputted into the system via a series of sliders.

Theater Lighting with Global Positioning

Utilizing a Raspberry Pi and a SparkFun GPS-RTK2 sensor, I attempted to build a system that would take GPS coordinates and auto-adjust a spotlight based on an actor's position on a stage. This ultimately ran into issues regarding poor signal within buildings, but for open-air stages such as concerts (rather than theater performances), this may still work. To the right is a preliminary sketch of a garment that would allow the sensor to be worn.

Rochester Rivers Fullstack Website - SWEN 261 Project

A website created for an imaginary non-profit organization (Rochester Rivers). The site featured two types of users - Admins and Helpers. Admins could manage items that the organization required within the store, and Helpers could purchase these items for the organization. Helpers had unique baskets (functionally equivalent to an online shopping cart), the contents of which would be preserved if the user exited the site.
